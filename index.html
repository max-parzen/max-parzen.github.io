Guiding Energy Storage Innovation with Energy System Modelling


Thesis Opportunities 20/21

Designing the next generation of energy storage is one of the most challenging task. It seems that only focusing on cost reduction is not enough to develop future competitive technology. To guide technology development we must look into the system-value of technology. A selection of Youtube videos and papers introduce you to this system-value idea:

  • Hirth 2019 "Renewable Power Economics.", Youtube. [Excellent video!]
  • Strbac et al. 2017 "Opportunities for Energy Storage: Assessing Whole-System Economic Benefits of Energy Storage in Future Electricity Systems."
  • Sisternes et al. 2016 "The value of energy storage in decarbonizing the electricity sector."
  • Bistline et al. 2020 "Energy storage in long-term system models: a review of considerations, best practices, and research needs."

For the cutting edge research on energy storage, I am willing to take 1-3 master students as co-supervisor. You will benefit from:

  • Working on projects that are planned for publication (maybe with your name on the paper)
  • Learning & applying fantastic energy system models in a short modelling seminar
  • Close collaboration with me

The offered topics include:

  • Review about common and exotic energy storage technologies (1x position)
  • Investigation about the impact of nonlinear energy storage characteristics on the system-value (1-2x positions)
  • Review about the existing energy data situation in Africa and pathways to improve it. (1x position)

The Model

The research will apply one of the best-performing open source energy system model PyPSA-Eur. An excellent video about how PyPSA-Eur works can be found HERE.

More suggested papers on energy system modelling:

  • Connolly et al. 2010 "A review of computer tools for analysing the integration of renewable energy into various energy systems."
  • Ringkjob et al. 2018 "A review of modelling tools for energy and electricity systems with large shares of variable renewable."
  • Pfenninger et al. 2014 "Energy systems modeling for twenty-first century energy challenges."
  • Neumann et al. 2020 "Costs of Regional Equity and Autarky in a Renewable European Power System."
  • Hörsch et al. 2017 "The role of spatial scale in joint optimisations of generation and transmission for European highly renewable scenarios."
  • Neumann et al. 2021 "The near-optimal feasible space of a renewable power system model."
  • Victoria et al. 2020 "Early decarbonisation of the European energy system pays off."

About Me

In my PhD research, I am guiding energy storage innovation with optimisation methods. It was found that cost minimising and profit maximising approaches fail to signal the future technology competitiveness. Hence, I am exploring the role of data-driven energy system models to assess the system-value of energy storage. Advanced tools and techniques are applied, such as open energy system models (PyPSA-Eur) and Machine Learning techniques.

I tapped with a Co-founding project in the food industry into the exciting world of start-ups. Even though I had to stop, the experience sparked some innovation projects such as VisViva! As a hobby, I am open to discuss or push your ideas/inventions to making them market-ready. Just drop me a message. I am happy to help! I like #AI #data #energy solutions.
